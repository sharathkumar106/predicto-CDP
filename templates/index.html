A brief introduction

Agriculture is one of the main economic activities of India. It contributes amply to the gross national income. Nowadays crops face many traits or diseases. Many crops are affected by the diseases due to environmental conditions. Hence crop disease prediction is an important task to the agricultural development in India. Internet of Things (IoT) network will help in real-time data collection. Machine learning algorithms give more precise predictions. The diseases in crops can be predicted and intimated to the farmer so that huge losses can be prevented.

In recent years, there has been a large-scale infection area of crop diseases and insect pest, which caused heavy economic losses to the farmers. Crop production losses due to pests and diseases are quite substantial, particularly in the Indian semi-arid weather conditions. We intend to use IoT and Machine Learning to acquire and analyse various data to predict the possibility of the diseases in crops. The idea is to train a machine to learn the pattern of temperature, soil composition, humidity, pest occurrence to identify the real-time data and classify them to predict the disease occurrence.

The objective is to identify a sequence of environmental conditions that takes place prior to a disease attack and learn the pattern to predict the disease with high accuracy. The diseases in crops can be predicted and intimated to the farmer so that huge losses can be prevented. The proposed work can also be extended to act as a guide for farmers like, which fertilizer can be used to overcome the disease problem and which crop is beneficial to sow in a specific weather condition.

Predictor Features

Internet of Things

It takes into consideration various environmental changes that lead to a crop disease. Real-time data is collected from sensors.

Machine Learning

ML models are used to analyse the data and predict the possibilities of disease.

Data-Centric

Historic data is used to train the model effectively. A range of values is mined for a pattern to contribute to result.
